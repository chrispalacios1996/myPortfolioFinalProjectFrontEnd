What is Tennis?

Tennis is a non-contact sport that was created in 1859. Originally thought to be from Birmingham,
England, Tennis is now a sport played worldwide by all levels and ages. Many believe that the
game was originally invented in the 12th century in France; where a ball was struck with the
palm of the hand, similar to handball. It can be played individually against another
opponent, or between two teams of two players.

Game Objective:

The objective of Tennis is very simple; a player must hit the ball in a way that his/her
opponent will not be able to return it. The player who is unable to return the ball
will not gain a point, while the other player will. In other sports such as hockey and
soccer, points increase by 1 every time a player scores; however, in Tennis that is not the
case. Every time a player wins a rally (tennis play), points will increase from
15 to 30, and 30 to 40. While the game of Tennis may look easy, it is definitely not.

How Can I Play Tennis?

In order to play Tennis you will need:

  • A friend
  • Two Tennis rackets (one for each person)
  • a Tennis court
  • A Tennis ball
  • Lots of energy

Side note:

When playing tennis, always be sure to carry a water bottle as this sport
is very exhausting. You will be running up and down and side to side
most of the time and will only receive a few 20 second breaks; these breaks
will be taken while standing.
